wobl bobl

Can machines create their own ecosystem without the need of human interference?

I wanted to tackle that question by creating robots that would interact on their own and generate ideas.

Research / Inspiration

I looked into Harvard's swarm robotics and Alonso Martinez's animated robots. Key features that I wanted to tackle were the ability to communicate wirelessly and autonomously, and convey personality and the illusion of being alive.

Process

First, I experiemted with a soft-bodied design, where the movement was driven by mini solenoids in the robots. Found materials, including socks, paper cups, and ping pong balls were used in the body.

I then moved onto creating a more rugged design with the option to toggle the settings with a built in button, which allowed for more thorough debugging and varied functionality.

Figuring Out IR Sending & Receiving

Arguably, the most difficult aspect of this project was figuring out the hardware. I decided to use infrared technology, as it was fast, low-fidelity, and would operate without needing the cloud.

The salt and pepper shakers were a perfect symbol for family conversations, and an opportunity for intervention.
